AI Smart Agriculture Robot

This project presents an AI-based smart agriculture robot designed to automate irrigation and weed control. It uses Artificial Intelligence, sensors, and IoT to improve crop health while reducing water usage and manual labor.

Problem

In Tunisia, most farms still rely on traditional agricultural methods. There are almost no smart robots used in daily farming, and irrigation is often done manually or based on fixed schedules. Farmers usually do not know exactly how much water each plant needs, which leads to over-watering in some areas and under-watering in others.

  • Most farms do not use smart or automated systems.
  • Farmers estimate water needs by experience, not data.
  • Water resources are limited, especially during drought seasons.
  • Irrigation systems waste water due to lack of monitoring.
  • Weed removal is done manually or by spraying chemicals everywhere.
  • There is no real-time data to help farmers make decisions.

Solution

This project provides a smart and practical solution adapted to Tunisian farms. Instead of relying on estimation or fixed irrigation schedules, the robot uses sensors and Artificial Intelligence to make decisions based on real plant needs. The system is designed to be affordable, easy to use, and suitable for small and medium farms commonly found in Tunisia.

  • The robot measures soil moisture to know exactly when plants need water.
  • Water is supplied only when necessary, reducing waste.
  • AI vision detects weeds and treats only the affected area.
  • Farmers do not need technical knowledge to use the system.
  • The system helps save water during dry and drought seasons.
  • Reduces manual labor and improves productivity.

Hardware

The robot uses an Arduino Mega for low-level control of motors, pumps, sensors, and relays. A Raspberry Pi 4 handles AI processing and computer vision using YOLO and OpenCV. The ESP32-CAM captures real-time images for weed detection. Environmental sensors measure soil moisture, temperature, humidity, light, and rain conditions. The irrigation and spraying systems operate automatically based on sensor data and AI decisions. Through Wi-Fi communication, the robot sends real-time data such as soil moisture, water usage, and system status to the farmer via an IoT dashboard or mobile application.

  • ESP32-CAM
  • Raspberry Pi 4
  • Arduino Mega
  • Soil Moisture Sensors
  • DHT22 Temperature & Humidity Sensor
  • Ultrasonic Sensors
  • Water Pump & Spray System
  • Wi-Fi Communication (IoT)

Artificial Intelligence

The Raspberry Pi runs a YOLO deep learning model trained to recognize weeds among crops. OpenCV processes camera images in real time. When a weed is detected, its position is calculated and sent to the Arduino, which activates the spraying or cutting mechanism only on the detected weed.

How It Works

1. The robot moves along the crop rows automatically.
2. Soil moisture, temperature, humidity, and light sensors collect data.
3. ESP32-CAM captures live images of the plants and soil surface.
4. Raspberry Pi processes images using YOLO to detect weeds.
5. If a weed is detected, the system calculates its position.
6. Arduino activates the spray or cutter only at the weed location.
7. If soil moisture is low, the irrigation pump is activated automatically.
8. All sensor data, water usage, and system status are sent via IoT to the farmer‚Äôs phone or dashboard in real time.
